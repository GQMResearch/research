2. Project Overview

What It Does

Altura is a multi-strategy yield vault on HyperEVM (Hyperliquid's EVM chain). Users deposit USDT0, receive vault shares (AVLT), and the protocol executes institutional-grade trading strategies in the background.

Key claim: 20% base APY from execution-based strategies — no token emissions, no farming mechanics.

Source: docs.altura.trade, @alturax

How It Works

  1. User deposits USDT0 → receives AVLT vault shares
  2. Altura deploys capital across 3 strategy pillars (see yield sources below)
  3. NAV/PPS (Price Per Share) updates on-chain — verifiable in real-time
  4. Yield accrues immediately after deposit
  5. Withdrawals processed through vault logic

Source: docs.altura.trade/howitworks

3. ⚠️ Where Does the Yield Come From?

This is the critical section. Altura claims ~20% base APY from 3 strategy pillars. Let's break each one down:

Strategy Pillar 1: Delta-Neutral Crypto Market Making

What it does: Provides bid/ask liquidity on selected venues, capturing spread-based revenue while hedging directional exposure.

How yield is generated:

  • Spread capture from quoting engine (bid-ask differential)
  • Liquidity provision fees
  • Opportunistic regime-aware adjustments

Risk controls claimed:

  • Inventory exposure limits
  • Volatility-based hedging
  • Automated kill-switches
  • Exchange/venue diversification

Risk assessment:

  • Market making can be very profitable but also risky in volatile conditions ⚠️
  • Inventory risk during flash crashes is real — delta-neutral is an ideal, not a guarantee
  • "Automated kill-switches" is good but execution during extreme volatility is the real test
  • We don't know which venues they trade on — could be Hyperliquid perps + other CEXs ⚠️

Source: docs.altura.trade/strategies

Strategy Pillar 2: Funding Rate & Basis Arbitrage

What it does: Captures yield from structural inefficiencies between spot, perp, and dated futures markets. Classic delta-neutral strategy.

How yield is generated:

  • Long spot / short perpetual → collect positive funding rates
  • Long perp / short futures → capture basis convergence
  • Cross-venue arbitrage

Risk controls claimed:

  • Funding persistence monitoring
  • Margin and liquidation buffers
  • Position size controls
  • Continuous rebalancing

Risk assessment:

  • This is a well-understood, proven strategy (Ethena uses a version of this) ✅
  • Works especially well in bull markets when funding rates are elevated
  • Risk: Negative funding periods can erode returns
  • Risk: Liquidation risk if margin buffers are insufficient during extreme moves ⚠️
  • Hyperliquid's on-chain perps give natural access to this trade

Source: docs.altura.trade/strategies

Strategy Pillar 3: Real-World Asset (RWA) Gold Trading

What it does: Asset-backed yield from gold trading activity — appears to involve trading physical/tokenized gold.

How yield is generated:

  • Gold spot/futures arbitrage
  • Asset-backed revenue from real economic activity

Risk assessment:

  • Most opaque of the three pillars ⚠️
  • Gold market is deep and liquid, which is good
  • But details on execution (which venues, what counterparties) are limited
  • Diversification benefit: uncorrelated to crypto strategies ✅

Source: docs.altura.trade/strategies

7. Governance & Security Architecture

Altura uses a 3-tier role system (better than most new protocols):

Role Can Do Cannot Do
Guardian Pause/unpause vault Move funds, change PPS
Operator Submit oracle updates, trigger harvests Transfer assets, pause
Timelock Admin Update configs (after delay) Bypass timelock, move funds

Key protections:

  • Permissioned transfers only (assets can only go to pre-approved contracts + keeper EOAs) ✅
  • Oracle validation with upper/lower bounds ✅
  • Enforced pause states ✅
  • Timelock on all config changes ✅

Source: docs.altura.trade/governance, docs.altura.trade/safety

⚠️ Concern: "Keeper EOAs" are whitelisted — these are externally owned accounts that can receive funds. This means the team can move funds to these addresses. This is standard for trading vaults (they need to move capital to execute strategies) but creates trust dependency.

9. Summary

Strengths

  1. Real yield sources — market making, funding arb, gold trading. No emissions. (docs)
  2. 5 audits including Sherlock — unusually thorough for a new protocol (docs)
  3. Strong governance architecture — timelocked admin, separated roles, permissioned transfers (docs)
  4. On-chain verifiability — NAV/PPS observable in real-time (docs)
  5. No leverage looping or circular incentives — sustainable model claim
  6. Pre-TGE bonus — 30% at TGE + 6mo vest aligns incentives (docs)

Weaknesses

  1. Anonymous team — no known identities, no disclosed investors ⚠️⚠️
  2. Very new — main vault live since ~Dec 2025, <3 months track record
  3. Active trading risk — market making can lose money, this is not lending
  4. HyperEVM chain risk — young chain, less battle-tested infrastructure
  5. Keeper EOA trust — team-controlled addresses can receive vault funds
  6. 20% base APY sustainability — unproven over full market cycle
  7. Gold strategy opacity — least transparent of the three pillars
  8. $3.8M TVL — very small, liquidity risk on exits

Verdict

Altura is an interesting protocol with genuinely differentiated yield strategies (not another lending aggregator). The audit coverage is surprisingly strong for its size. However, the anonymous team + active trading strategies + very new chain create a risk profile that demands careful position sizing.

The 75-97% headline APR is misleading — ~55-77% of that is temporary Merkl pre-TGE incentives ending April 30. The real question is whether the 20% base APY from trading strategies is sustainable.

Suitable for: Small, speculative allocations only. Not for core positions. Position sizing: Max 2-5% of stablecoin portfolio given risk profile. Monitor: NAV/PPS trends on-chain, team doxxing updates, post-April APY when incentives end.
