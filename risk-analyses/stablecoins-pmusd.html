PMUSD (M^0 Protocol USD)

Overview

  • Type: Fiat-backed stablecoin
  • Issuer: M^0 Protocol
  • Backing: T-bills and cash equivalents
  • Market Cap: Smaller (~$50-100M range)
  • Chains: Ethereum
  • Website: https://m0.org
  • Status: ⚠️ NEWER PROTOCOL

Mechanism

M^0 Protocol

  • Decentralized stablecoin protocol
  • Minters stake collateral
  • Validators verify reserves
  • Novel governance model

How PMUSD is Minted

  1. Approved minters deposit collateral
  2. Collateral held by approved custodians
  3. Validators verify reserves
  4. PMUSD minted against verified collateral

Risk Factors

Strengths

  • Designed for decentralization
  • Multiple minters (not single issuer)
  • T-bill backing (safe collateral)
  • Innovative approach to stablecoin issuance

Risks

  1. Newer Protocol: Limited track record
  2. Complexity: Novel minter/validator system
  3. Smaller TVL: Less battle-tested
  4. Liquidity: May have thinner liquidity

Research Required

  • Audit status
  • Minter quality
  • Validator reliability
  • Insurance/backstops

Summary

PMUSD is an interesting newer stablecoin with a decentralized minter/validator model. T-bill backing is conservative, but the protocol itself is novel and less proven. Higher yields reflect higher risk and adoption incentives. Suitable for smaller allocations with higher risk tolerance.
