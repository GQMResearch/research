USDT (Tether)

Overview

  • Type: Fiat-backed stablecoin
  • Issuer: Tether Limited
  • Backing: USD reserves + commercial paper + T-bills
  • Market Cap: ~$95B+ (largest stablecoin)
  • Chains: Ethereum, Tron, many others
  • Website: https://tether.to

Backing & Reserves

Collateral Composition (as reported)

  • US Treasury bills (majority)
  • Cash and cash equivalents
  • Secured loans
  • Other investments
  • Reduced commercial paper exposure

Reserve Transparency

  • Quarterly attestations (not full audits)
  • Auditor: BDO Italia
  • Less transparent than USDC
  • No real-time proof of reserves

Redemption

  • 1:1 for qualified entities
  • $100,000 minimum
  • KYC required
  • Not retail-accessible directly

Controversy & History

Historical Concerns

  • 2019: NY AG settlement ($18.5M)
  • Reserve composition questions
  • Banking relationship opacity
  • Not fully audited (attestations only)

Current Status

  • Reduced commercial paper
  • Increased T-bill holdings
  • Still not fully transparent
  • Profitable (earns yield on reserves)

Risk Factors

Strengths

  • Highest liquidity
  • Longest track record (2014)
  • Never broken peg significantly
  • Most widely accepted

Risks

  1. Transparency: Attestations, not audits
  2. Jurisdiction: BVI-based, less regulated
  3. Blacklisting: Can freeze addresses
  4. Counterparty: Reserve quality questions

Risk Scores

Category Score Notes
Backing 3.5/5 Less transparent, improving
Peg Stability 4.5/5 Excellent 10-year record
Issuer 3.5/5 Offshore, transparency concerns
Censorship Resistance 3/5 Can blacklist
Overall 4/5

Summary

USDT is the most liquid stablecoin with the longest track record. Transparency concerns exist but it has never significantly broken peg in 10 years. Suitable for trading and short-term use. For long-term holdings, USDC may be preferable due to better transparency.
