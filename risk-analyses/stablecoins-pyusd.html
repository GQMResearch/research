PYUSD (PayPal USD)

Overview

  • Type: Fiat-backed stablecoin
  • Issuer: Paxos (for PayPal)
  • Backing: 1:1 USD reserves
  • Market Cap: ~$500M+
  • Chains: Ethereum, Solana
  • Website: https://www.paypal.com/pyusd

Backing & Reserves

Collateral Composition

  • US Treasury bills
  • Cash equivalents
  • 100% backed at all times
  • Monthly attestations

Reserve Transparency

  • Regulated by NYDFS
  • Paxos trust company issues
  • Monthly attestations available
  • Same standard as USDP (Pax Dollar)

Redemption

  • 1:1 via PayPal/Venmo
  • Requires PayPal account
  • Instant for PayPal users
  • Institutional via Paxos

Issuer Analysis

Paxos (Issuer)

  • Founded: 2012
  • Regulated: NYDFS Trust Company
  • Track record: Also issues USDP, BUSD (discontinued)
  • Strong compliance focus

PayPal (Brand)

  • Fortune 500 company
  • Regulated financial institution
  • Massive user base
  • Traditional finance backing

Risk Factors

Strengths

  • PayPal backing (mainstream credibility)
  • NYDFS regulated
  • Paxos track record
  • Transparent reserves

Risks

  1. Centralization: Can freeze/blacklist addresses
  2. PayPal Dependent: Brand risk tied to PayPal
  3. Newer: Launched August 2023
  4. Adoption: Still growing in DeFi

Blacklisting

  • OFAC compliant (like USDC)
  • Can and will freeze addresses
  • PayPal's compliance standards apply

DeFi Integration

Where It's Used

  • Curve pools (paired with crvUSD, USDC)
  • Growing DeFi presence
  • Aave considering integration
  • Solana DeFi

Yields

  • Higher yields than USDC in some pools
  • Protocols incentivizing adoption
  • May compress as adoption grows

Risk Scores

Summary

PYUSD is a credible fiat-backed stablecoin from PayPal/Paxos with strong regulatory backing. Similar risk profile to USDC with added mainstream brand credibility. Newer (2023) but from proven issuers. Higher yields currently due to incentivized adoption. Good for yield strategies accepting centralization risk.
