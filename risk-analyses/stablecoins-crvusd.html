crvUSD

Overview

  • Type: CDP stablecoin with LLAMMA
  • Issuer: Curve Finance
  • Backing: Over-collateralized crypto
  • Market Cap: ~$500M+
  • Chains: Ethereum (native)
  • Website: https://crvusd.curve.fi

Mechanism

LLAMMA (Lending-Liquidating AMM Algorithm)

  • Unique "soft liquidation" mechanism
  • As collateral value drops, gradually converts to crvUSD
  • When price recovers, converts back to collateral
  • Reduces hard liquidation risk

How It Differs from DAI

Feature crvUSD DAI
Liquidation Soft (gradual) Hard (instant)

Backing & Reserves

Collateral Types

  • ETH and ETH LSDs
  • WBTC
  • Other approved collateral
  • Over-collateralized design

Peg Mechanism

  • PegKeepers maintain peg
  • Can mint/burn to stabilize
  • Arbitrage incentives

Risk Factors

Strengths

  • Innovative liquidation mechanism
  • Curve's security track record
  • Multiple audits (ChainSecurity, MixBytes)
  • Growing adoption

Risks

  1. Novel Mechanism: LLAMMA is new, less battle-tested
  2. Soft Liquidation Loss: Can still lose value in volatility
  3. Oracle Risk: Internal oracle design
  4. Newer: ~2 years vs DAI's 7 years

LLAMMA Risks

  • Soft liquidation still results in losses
  • "De-liquidation" isn't guaranteed
  • Complex mechanism may have edge cases

Summary

crvUSD brings innovation to CDP stablecoins with LLAMMA's soft liquidation. Backed by Curve's excellent security track record and multiple audits. Less battle-tested than DAI but showing strong performance. Good for yield strategies where Curve integration provides benefits.
