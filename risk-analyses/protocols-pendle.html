Economic Model

Yield Source

  • Trading fees on PT/YT swaps
  • Underlying yield from assets (passed to YT holders)
  • PENDLE emissions for liquidity mining

How Pendle Works

  1. Yield-bearing assets split into PT (Principal Token) and YT (Yield Token)
  2. PT = fixed yield (buy at discount, redeem at par at maturity)
  3. YT = variable yield (receives all yield until maturity)
  4. AMM enables trading between these components

Token (PENDLE)

  • Governance token
  • vePENDLE for boosted rewards and fee share
  • 2% annual inflation cap

Sustainability

  • Real yield from swap fees
  • Underlying assets generate real yield
  • Emissions controlled via vePENDLE

PT-Specific Risks

When using Pendle PT strategies:

  1. Duration Risk: Locked until maturity - early exit may result in loss
  2. Underlying Asset Risk: PT value depends on underlying (e.g., sUSDai depends on DAI)
  3. Smart Contract Risk: Both Pendle contracts AND underlying protocol
  4. Yield Compression: Fixed APY set at purchase - if rates rise, you're locked in

PT Risk by Underlying

Underlying Additional Risk
sUSDai Low - DAI savings rate, MakerDAO backing
sUSDe Medium - Ethena's delta-neutral strategy
Restaking (eETH, etc.) Medium-High - Additional smart contract layers

Summary

Pendle is the leading yield tokenization protocol with solid security practices. Multiple audits, clean track record since V2, and a well-designed economic model. PT strategies offer fixed yields but carry duration and underlying asset risk. Best used for shorter maturities on high-conviction underlyings (DAI, USDC-based).
